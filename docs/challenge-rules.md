# Fitness Challenge Rules

## Schedule
- Baseline week: **April 27, 2026 – May 3, 2026**
- Week 1 start: **May 4, 2026**
- End date: **June 4, 2026**

## Goal
Make the challenge fair for both:
- people who already train consistently
- people who are just getting started

## What counts
Participants can log:
- active minutes
- steps
- whether they completed a workout
- whether they completed mobility/stretching

## Fairness philosophy
The challenge is **not** about who is already the fittest. It is about:
- showing up
- building habits
- improving relative to your own baseline

## Daily point rules
### Active minutes
- 10–19 = 1
- 20–29 = 2
- 30–44 = 3
- 45–59 = 4
- 60+ = 5

### Workout done
- yes = 2
- no = 0

### Steps
- 6,000+ = 1
- 8,000+ = 2
- 10,000+ = 3

### Mobility/stretching
- 10+ minutes = 1

### Daily max
- capped at 10 points

## Weekly bonus rules
### Consistency
- 3 active days = 3 points
- 5 active days = 6 points
- 6+ active days = 8 points

### Improvement vs baseline
#### Active minutes
- +10% = 3
- +20% = 5
- +30% = 7

#### Steps
- +10% = 2
- +20% = 4

### Personal best week
- best challenge week so far = 2

## Active day definition
An active day is:
- 20+ active minutes, or
- workout completed

## Recommended honor rules
- log by the end of the next day
- one workout credit per day
- no retroactive edits after the weekly cutoff unless the group agrees
- use wearable data if available, but manual entry is allowed

## Why the cap matters
Without a cap, already-fit participants could dominate on volume alone. The daily cap keeps the challenge competitive and emphasizes consistency.
